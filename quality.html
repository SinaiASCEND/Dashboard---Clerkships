How this connects. Element 9.4 is direct observation of clinical skills. The monitoring matrix answers did observation happen (survey, all clerkships); the Direct Observation mode answers how students performed when observed (SP-OSCE item-level), for the clerkships that run SP-OSCEs. Both layers are cohort-level aggregates.

The five SP OSCE stations

Each card reports station-level psychometrics (cohort mean, SD, score distribution) and the item-level mean (% of students earning full credit). The lowest-scoring Morchand checklist skills are surfaced as candidate targets for standard-setting and station revision.

NBME Physician Tasks — performance × station

Every Morchand checklist item is mapped to one NBME competency, which rolls up to its NBME Physician Task (domain + subdomain) and to its NBME Domain. Switch the grouping between domain, task, or competency; tick Show full framework to expose competencies that no station currently assesses (a coverage-gap view). Each cell is the mean item difficulty (% full credit) within a station; the small figure is the contributing item count, and the right-hand column pools all stations.

Cells = mean % full credit · small figure = item count
Reading the map. Hatched cells mean the station does not assess that task. A task assessed in only one station (e.g., Death & Dying, Pharmacotherapy) signals where the construct lives in the curriculum; a task assessed everywhere (Fostering the Relationship, Patient Safety) supports cross-station equating. Low-scoring, widely-assessed tasks are the strongest candidates for a shared minimum-performance floor.

Morchand checklist skills — performance × station

The Morchand checklist skills, rolled up from the station section headers so they read consistently across cases. Use this alongside the NBME view: the NBME framework gives an external, accreditation-facing vocabulary; the Morchand skills map directly onto how the stations and rubrics are authored.

Across the two SP stations of a clerkship

A clerkship runs two SP encounters. This view places them side by side so a domain or task that is consistently harder at one station becomes visible — useful for balancing rubric stringency and confirming both stations sample comparable competencies.

Item explorer

Every scored checklist item, with its Morchand skill, NBME Physician Task & competency, and difficulty. Filter and sort to triage the hardest items, audit a single competency across stations, or pull the evidence behind a standard-setting decision. Items below 60% full credit are flagged.

Metric. Per item, % full credit = the proportion of the cohort earning full marks (the classical item difficulty / p-value), read directly from the Elevate Healthcare Case-Item Analysis. Station mean & SD are from the Score Distribution reports. Domain and task cells are unweighted means of their constituent item p-values.
Frameworks. NBME Physician Tasks/Competencies © 2020 FSMB & NBME. A Physician Task is the NBME domain and subdomain combined; each item is mapped to one competency, from which its task is inherited.
